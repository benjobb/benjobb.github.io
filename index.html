High-strain dynamic load testing (PDA Tests)

Also called PDA tests, these estimate the static capacity, load-settlement behavior, pile stresses, and structural integrity of all types of deep pile foundations. These are conducted and monitored by a team of our highly experienced pile testing and civil engineers. These are done with the Pile Driving Analyzer of Pile Dynamics, Inc. We guarantee our tests include:

  • A geotechnically credible signal matching analysis solution with CAPWAP;
  • Interpretation of CAPWAP results on the acceptability of piles;
  • Use of our extensive history of PDA testing in evaluating piles;

Low-strain pile integrity testing (PIT)

The Low-strain pile integrity test, also called the PIT Test, is a quick and cost-effective method to evaluate the shaft integrity of concrete piles. Testing is done with the Pile Integrity Tester of Pile Dynamics, Inc. It determines the pile continuity, location of possible defects, and consistency of the concrete. It is performed by affixing an accelerometer to the pile top and striking the pile with an instrumented hand-held hammer.

  • Requires minimal preparation.
  • Can be done on a large number of piles at a project.
  • Relatively quick and inexpensive.
  • Can even evaluate length and quality of existing foundations.

Cross-hole sonic logging

The Crosshole Sonic Logging (CSL) method is used to evaluate the quality of the concrete of deep foundations. Testing is performed with a Cross-Hole Analyzer (CHA) from Pile Dynamics, Inc. During the test a transmitter is lowered down one of the tubes and sends a high frequency signal to a receiver inserted in another tube. The transmitter and receiver move down each pair of tubes, scanning the entire length of the shaft for imperfections and voids in the concrete.

  • Relatively simple interpretation of defects.
  • Can be done on very long piles with minimal challenges.
  • Can determine extent and depth of anomalies with reasonable accuracy.

3D TOMOGRAPHIC ANALYSIS (PDI-TOMO)

Tomography or tomographic analysis is a mathematical procedure that is applied to CSL data that combines first arrival time (FAT) data from the scans of all pairs of CSL tubes to provide a 2D or 3D visual estimate to the extent of irregularities or defects. Specifically, it allows for the lateral and vertical extent of possible defects/flaws to be examined. This is done with the PDI-TOMO software from Pile Dynamics, Inc
